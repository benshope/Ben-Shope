About

2013 – PRESENT

Ben currently lives in Mountain View, CA and works as a software engineer. His favorite new technologies are WebApp2, AngularJS, and PyOpenCL.

2011 – 2012

Gardening, reading, and living - in Columbus, OH of all places. After overcoming my coastal fear of the middle of the country, it was all actually very pleasant.

2007 – 2011

Ben was educated at Colgate University.  In college, Ben was a staff writer for the newspaper, a member of the Colgate cross country team, and a writer for the monthly satire publication.  He spent a semester abroad in Florence, Italy studying wine, fashion, and wine.  Over the summers, Ben interned on a feature film and learned kiteboarding.

2004 – 2007

After middle school, Ben attended The Hotchkiss School and worked at his family’s environmental center.  At Hotchkiss, Ben played club tennis in the winter, was an active member of the debate team, and founded the Hotchkiss chess (and bughouse) club.

1989 – 2004

Ben was born in Greenwich, CT in 1989. He attended the Whitby School and later the Greenwich Country Day School. Outside of academics, Ben participated in several regattas in Optimists (small, bathtub-like boats); this was the peak of his sailing career.

Think

Vision couples discovery with resolution, serving as a catalyst for change. Charting a new course requires that we identify the true nature of a product opportunity. At the beginning, it's not what we design, but why we're designing the product. Our work provides insight and context for the product development process. We examine the product user within the broader ecosystem, we ask questions, and we do product development research in search of deeper meaning that leads to heightened experience.

Build

The best thinking comes from people who are accountable for, and part of, the making of the thing. Insights, vision, and intent are rendered powerless without proper and thorough execution. Creative minds become more innovative when they physically construct the things that they envision because the art of building means that we learn from doing. At Ben Shope we are focused on making things, solving practical problems, creating wonderful experiences, and making useful things beautiful.

Transform

Design has the power to transform thinking, behavior, experiences, and outcomes. Our commitment to product transformation means we are accountable and involved from the initial briefing, through creation, to the final evaluation of products that we design. At the outset of every product development project, we collaborate with our clients to clearly define the desired transformations. The design path is guided by these objectives, and we hold ourselves accountable for achieving them.
